01 the thesis

the thesis

Most software companies want to be the platform. The ecosystem. The one-stop shop. frgmt goes the other direction.

Make the smallest useful thing. Make it well. Give it away. Let it connect to whatever else people are building.

A tool that does one job. A game that takes twenty minutes. A tutorial that teaches one concept. A protocol that slots into someone else’s stack. None of these try to be the whole — they’re pieces, and that’s the point.

the bet — small, thoughtful pieces compound. A hundred good fragments beat one mediocre platform.

03 how frgmt works

the principles

  1. 01

    Ethics over profit.

    If a choice makes more money but makes the product worse or the user more exploited, it doesn’t get made. Boring. Non-negotiable.

  2. 02

    Accessibility is a feature, not a checkbox.

    If it doesn’t work with a screen reader, on a cheap laptop, for someone who’s never opened a terminal — it’s not done.

  3. 03

    Inclusivity without performance.

    No rainbow logo in June and bad behavior in July. Build things that work for everyone, and hire that way too.

  4. 04

    Small over scalable.

    A fragment that does one thing beautifully beats a framework nobody asked for.

  5. 05

    Open by default.

    Source available, standards preferred, lock-in avoided.

  6. 06

    Done is better than perfect.

    Ship the fragment. Polish it later. Other fragments are waiting.

04 counterweights

what frgmt isn’t

  • A startup. No VC, no exit, no growth-at-all-costs theater.
  • A platform. Fragments don’t aspire to be ecosystems.
  • A bootcamp or content mill. Education is a side effect of making real things.
  • Serious in the self-important sense. Dorky? yes. Solemn? no.
05 the shape of the team

one + a piece

Right now it’s one person — the founder and lead. That’s fine. frgmt is designed to work at one person and keep working as more show up.

Each contributor brings a fragment. The studio is the sum. When people join, they don’t become cogs in a roadmap — they bring what they’re good at, make fragments of their own, and frgmt gets bigger the way a mosaic gets bigger: piece by piece, not by dilution.
